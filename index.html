About me

I am a Colombian Economist and Physicist based in Chicago. I work as a pre-doctoral research assistant for Professor James Robinson. In Economics, my interests lie in the intersection between Economic History, Political Economy, and Econometrics. My research revolves around war and power, in particular, I have conducted research about the Colombian armed conflict and the long-run effects of the 13th-century Mongol Conquests. In Physics, my field is Cosmology, specifically, my main interest is the Hubble Tension. My research here has focused on producing methods to measure extragalactic distances and estimating the expansion rate of the universe.
